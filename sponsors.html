The Korea-Japan Workshop on Ocean Color (KJWOC) and the Asian Workshop on Ocean Color (AWOC) are held annually, rotating among host countries across Asia. In 2026, we are honored to host this joint event in Toyama, Japan.

Previous Host Institutions: 2023 Japan (ISEE, Nagoya University), 2024 Indonesia (Udayana University), 2025 Korea (KIOST)

【Technological Scope: Ocean Color Science】

Ocean color remote sensing is a technology used to observe the components of light reflected from the ocean surface. By analyzing this "ocean color" in detail, it is possible to remotely measure essential elements for understanding the marine environment, such as phytoplankton (chlorophyll-a), suspended sediments, and dissolved organic matter.

The workshop encompasses a wide array of marine and coastal observation platforms—ranging from satellites in space and highly mobile aircraft to state-of-the-art drones for precision near-surface monitoring. Our scope extends beyond fundamental research to include practical applications and social implementations such as:

  • 🤖Advanced Data Analysis with AI and Machine Learning
  • 🐟Fisheries Support and Environmental Disaster Monitoring
  • 🌱Seagrass Bed Assessment and Blue Carbon Applications
  • 🚀Utilizing data by satellite sensors (e.g., GCOM-C/SGLI)
  • 🛰️Current Status of Ocean Observation Missions
  • 🎯Sensor Calibration and Validation
  • 💼Commercial Applications by Private Enterprises

【History and Significance: Over 20 Years of Connecting Asia】

The Korea-Japan Workshop on Ocean Color (KJWOC) started in 2003 in Nagasaki, Japan. For over 20 years, Japan and South Korea have taken turns to host the event, fostering a robust network of marine research in Asia. Since 2011, the workshop has expanded its reach by incorporating the Asian Workshop on Ocean Color (AWOC), welcoming participants from Southeast Asian nations. Today, it serves as a premier platform for participants to exchange cutting-edge information on ocean color remote sensing in the Asia-Pacific region.
