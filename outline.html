Workshop Overview

Asia’s Premier Marine Remote Sensing Hub with Over 20 Years of History

This workshop originated from the "Korea-Japan Workshop on Ocean Color (KJWOC)," which began in Nagasaki in 2003. For over two decades, Japan and South Korea have hosted the event alternately, building a robust network for marine research across Asia. In 2011, the "Asian Workshop on Ocean Color (AWOC)" was launched, incorporating Southeast Asian nations, and has been run concurrently with KJWOC. Today, KJWOC/AWOC has evolved into the most significant community for marine remote sensing in the Asia-Pacific region.

Diverse Technologies and Social Implementation

Ocean color remote sensing is a technique to observe light reflected from the sea surface. By analyzing "ocean color," we can remotely measure essential components for understanding the marine environment, such as phytoplankton (chlorophyll-a), suspended sediment, and dissolved organic matter.
This workshop provides participants with opportunities to present their latest studies on marine and coastal environments while covering a wide range of observation technologies, from satellites in space to highly mobile aircraft and drones, some cutting-edge innovation which is capable of precise near-field observations. Our discussions extend beyond basic marine research to include practical social implementation of technology, as outlined below:

  • 🤖 Advanced Data Analysis with AI and Machine LearningLeveraging AI algorithms to highly automate the detection of long-term environmental trends and anomalous phenomena from ocean color data.
  • 🐟 Fisheries Support and Environmental Disaster MonitoringEstablishing early detection systems for red tides and water pollution, contributing to the mitigation of damage to coastal fisheries and adaptation to climate change.
  • 🌱 Seagrass Bed Assessment and Blue Carbon ApplicationsPerforming precise mapping of seagrass beds (such as eelgrass) using optical sensors to quantify carbon sequestration capacity (blue carbon).
  • 🚀 Utilizing data by satellite sensors (e.g., GCOM-C/SGLI)Enhancing water quality and coastal mapping technologies by utilizing satellite observation data such as JAXA's "Shikisai" (GCOM-C).
  • 🛰️ Current Status of Ocean Observation MissionsReviewing and analyzing the operational status and acquired data of major Earth observation satellites to present challenges and directions for next-generation, wide-area ocean monitoring systems.
  • 🎯 Sensor Calibration and ValidationCorrecting the degradation of satellite sensors over time through cross-referencing with in-situ field measurement data (calibration) to ensure the long-term reliability and accuracy of observational data.
  • 💼 Commercial Applications by Private EnterprisesPromoting the practical implementation of data in the maritime business by providing high-precision ocean data via APIs, thereby facilitating tasks such as site selection for offshore wind power generation and the formulation of efficient shipping routes.
